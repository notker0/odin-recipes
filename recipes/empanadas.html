Empanadas

Description

An empanada is a type of baked or fried turnover consisting of pastry and filling, common in Spanish, other Southern European, Latin American, and the Philippine cultures. It's typically made with cheese, chicken, or beef filling, but anything be stuffed in this tasty pastel.

This recipe will be covering how to make a fried empanada, but several other guides like this one exist all over the internet. Let's get started!

Ingredients
  • Empanada Dough
  • Filling (Cheese, Beef, Chicken)
  • Flour
  • Vegetable Oil
Steps
  1. Prepare surface with flour and place empanada dough on top.
  2. Put chosen filling in the middle of the dough and fold one side to meet with the other, hotdog style.
  3. Use fork or pressing utensil to knead together the edge seam of the empanada, bringing it together and securing the filling inside.
  4. Stab through the middle of the empanada with your fork to make 3-4 small holes in the center.
  5. Flip the empanada and repeat step 3 & 4.
  6. Repeat for all empanadas that you're frying.
  7. Heat vegetable oil in a pan at medium-high heat.
  8. Once hot, put 2-3 empanadas in at a time to fry them. They cook quickly so only about 1-2 minutes per side and they should be done.
  9. Take out of pan and serve.
